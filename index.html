Landlord Reliability Scorecard

Prepared by: Ravneet Kaur Ranu

Explore the Journey

Introduction

It can be unexpectedly stressful to find safe and reliable housing as a student.

I was just out of town when I went for some new digs near my college and I didn’t find anywhere close to my university. Many landlords who had initially validated my move-in later cancelled without warning. In one instance, I submitted all my required paperwork and was only one week from transferring when the landlord canceled without a clear explanation. It left me utterly adrift.

These happened during a phase I was still thinking about: renters — especially students, and newcomers — had very little ownership over the housing process and lack protections. Listing platforms like Kijiji and Facebook Marketplace give you access to listings, but that does not provide any clear transparency about landlord action.

The Core Problem: Renting becomes an offer that appears to be a bet, where people have no hope things will go right.

The anxiety of uncertainty in the rental market.

Research

Primary Research

Empathy Interview

I interviewed another international student who recently sought housing. Her experiences mirrored mine:

  • Official move-ins undone overnight.
  • Unreliable communication.
  • Landlords avoiding accountability.
"A wish for a service that displays actual information beforehand, not after purchasing something."

Secondary Research

Market Analysis

News articles, reports, and social media forums confirmed this is a systemic issue:

  • CBC News: Dramatic upsurge in rental scams and last-minute cancellations.
  • Reddit Forums: Students experiencing surprise cancellations.
  • Systemic Issue: Lack of transparency creates a power imbalance.

Analysis

Root Cause Analysis (Fishbone)

Visualizing the root causes of last-minute cancellations and renter uncertainty.

Point of View Statement

“Tenants have a need for a transparent and trustworthy way to check landlords’ behavior and reliability before agreeing to a rental contract, as they are able to avoid last-minute cancellations and unsafe or unstable housing situations.”

Final HMW Question

“How might we surface a landlord’s verified reliability history in one trustworthy snapshot at the moment of decision?”

Ideation

From 30 divergent ideas to one focused solution.

1

Divergent Thinking

Brainstormed 30 unique ideas including transparency tools, blockchain verification, and AI summaries.

2

Convergent Thinking

Used a Decision Matrix to evaluate ideas based on feasibility, impact, and innovation.

3

Selection

Landlord Reliability Scorecard was chosen for the highest balance of feasibility and impact.

The Solution

Landlord Reliability Scorecard

A digital profile system that operates like a “credit score” for landlords.

Verified Identity

Confirms government ID and property ownership to prevent scams.

Reliability Score

0–100 score based on cancellation rates and behavior patterns.

Cancellation Tracking

Records and flags repeat offenders automatically.

AI Summaries

One-click clarity on reviews instead of overwhelming lists.

Reflection

This whole creative problem-solving cycle taught me about structured creativity translating personal frustration into an innovation opportunity. It’s a stress-ridden experience and housing has soon crystallized itself into a design challenge that centers on empathy, research, and analysis.

Performing an empathy interview was one of the most illuminating interventions. Hearing people’s complaints and frustrations validated that the issue was valid, emotional, and widespread. When I listened to other people saying it, it made me realize that creativity should always start with understanding people, and never jump on to solving problems.

The final concept—the Landlord Reliability Scorecard—is all what I understand: empathy, creativity, systemic analysis, understanding of a larger narrative, thinking about the full humanity. If it worked, this solution could put thousands of people in a better spot to rent, one way or another.
